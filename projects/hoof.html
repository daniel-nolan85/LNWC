Why Is This Program So Important?

Deer are the principle cause of an estimated 30 million dollars a year in crop damage in North Carolina. Deer are especially prevalent in our heavily agricultural counties to the north and west of Lake Norman. Under a depredation permit, farmers are allowed to reduce herds by as many deer as they choose on lands that they own or lease for growing crops. But these permits are only valid during the warm Spring and Summer crop growing seasons, when the combination of temperature, transportation, and lack of seasonal processors renders most of the venison unfit for consumption. Under a NC WRC program, hunters may take those same antlerless deer during the Fall hunting season and donate them for food distribution to individuals and families in need.

How It Works

During deer hunting season, we accept deer donations to a processing facility where it will be processed and packaged. LNWC volunteers will pick up the venison for distribution to partner agencies across the four county service area. This venison will provide a nutritious source of protein to families facing food insecurity, while helping farmers manage deer.

FAQ

Who Can Participate?

Farmers, Hunters, Individuals, Civic Clubs, Church Ministry Groups, Relief Organizations and Businesses

What is Your Purpose?

  • To ensure healthy deer populations
  • To assist with reduction of corp damage on farms
  • To support individuals suffering from food insecurity
  • To build community relations through giving back

Where Does The Food Go?

All of the donated venison goes to relief organizations in our local communities

How Can I Participate?

  • Spread the word in your community
  • Donate to help offset processing and storage cost
  • Host a fundraiser for the program
  • Donate legally harvested deer at participating sites
  • Provide access to land for approved hunters
  • Volunteer to distribute processed venison to relief organizations
